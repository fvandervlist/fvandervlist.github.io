Profile

I’m a researcher, writer, graphic designer, and educator specialising in digital media and technology. I’m an Assistant Professor and programme coordinator for the Master’s Cultural Data & AI in the Department of Media Studies at the University of Amsterdam (UvA), Faculty of Humanities. I co-direct the international App Studies Initiative (ASI) and am affiliated with the Digital Methods Initiative (DMI), the Public Data Lab, and the University of Amsterdam’s School for Cultural Analysis (ASCA) at the Amsterdam Institute for Humanities Research (AIHR).

🔍Platforms, Data, & Society My research focuses on the critical study of digital platforms and apps, their ecosystems, data, and artificial intelligence (AI) in culture and society. I’ve made significant contributions to the field, with my research being published or forthcoming in leading scholarly journals, such as Big Data & Society, New Media & Society, Social Media + Society, Internet Policy Review, AI & Society: Knowledge, Culture and Communication, Convergence, Internet Histories, and Computational Culture. My research has been funded by the German Research Foundation (DFG), the Dutch Research Council (NWO), and the UK’s Economic and Social Research Council (ESRC), amongst others.

🕓Prior to my current position, I was a postdoctoral Researcher in the focus area ‘Governing the Digital Society’ (GDS) at Utrecht University (UU) and the Collaborative Research Centre 1187 ‘Media of Cooperation’ at the University of Siegen, funded by the German Research Foundation (DFG). I’ve also taught as a Lecturer in New Media and Digital Culture and Digital Methods at the University of Amsterdam, Utrecht University, and the Utrecht Data School (UDS).

🎓I received my PhD from Utrecht University in 2022. The title of my PhD thesis monograph is ‘The Platform as Ecosystem’ and it was jointly supervised by Prof. José van Dijck at Utrecht University and Prof. Carolin Gerlitz at the University of Siegen, Germany. My empirical research extensively examines intricate governance and power dynamics within digital platforms and ecosystems, providing critical empirical, theoretical, and methodological insights into the complexities of platform governance. In addition to my academic pursuits, I have valuable professional experience as a graphic (information) designer, combining research and design expertise.

🔍Research

🔍My research is centred around the critical study of digital platforms and apps, their ecosystems, data, and artificial intelligence (AI) in culture and society. Spanning interdisciplinary fields like Internet Studies, Platform Studies, App Studies, Software Studies, and Critical Data Studies, his work encompasses foundational investigations into concepts such as ‘platformisation’, ‘appification’, partnership strategies, digital infrastructure, platform governance, and power dynamics in the digital ‘ecosystem’, including methodological frameworks and tools. Leveraging innovative methodologies and digital tools, he critically analyses and visualises the materiality of, and intricate relationships among, digital platforms, apps, users, and social and cultural processes, providing critical insights into their social, cultural, and political-economic dimensions.

My research contributions cover diverse areas, including foundational empirical studies of the ‘industrialisation’ and political economy of AI and cloud infrastructure (‘Big AI’), and power dynamics in the global ‘audience data economy’ and the digital marketing and advertising landscape. I have also analysed the pivotal role of partnership strategies and ‘API governance’ in Facebook’s platform ecosystem evolution, and power dynamics within the online platform ecosystem. Furthermore, I investigate the global culture and economy of apps, pioneering research methodologies and tools for app studies. For instance, my work in this area has addressed the global rise and political economy of ‘super-apps’ (‘super-appification’), the global landscape of COVID-19 apps and app store governance in the fight against the global coronavirus pandemic, and includes a foundational conceptual and methodological framework for historical platform and app studies (‘platform historiography’).

📚I’ve (co-)authored and edited over 30 scholarly publications (h-index: 13) in leading scholarly journals in the interdisciplinary field of Media Studies, including Big Data & Society (interdisciplinary social sciences), New Media & Society (sociology), and Social Media + Society (communication), as well as specialised journals, such as Internet Policy Review (internet regulation), AI & Society: Knowledge, Culture and Communication (artificial intelligence), Convergence (media studies), Internet Histories and TMG – Journal for Media History (media history), Surveillance & Society (surveillance studies), and Computational Culture (software studies). 🗓Additionally, I've actively engaged in more than 80 international academic conferences, workshops, and data ‘sprints’, including the annual Digital Methods Summer and Winter Schools and annual meetings of the Association for Internet Researchers (AoIR) and International Communication Association (ICA). 💲Moreover, I’ve contributed to securing research funding, including grants from national research councils like the German Research Foundation (DFG) and the UK’s Economic and Social Research Council (ESRC).

🧩I’ve developed various open-access research data sets, software tools, and teaching resources for new media, platform, and app studies research that are openly available and used across educational institutions. I actively participate in expert and policy meetings that focus on online platforms and the digital economy (such as at the European Union, Netherlands Authority for Consumers & Markets (ACM), the Rathenau Instituut, and for the Dutch political party D66).

🎓In 2022, I defended my PhD thesis, titled ‘The Platform as Ecosystem: Configurations and Dynamics of Governance and Power’, at Utrecht University. My empirical research extensively examines the intricate governance and power dynamics within digital platforms and ecosystems, providing critical insights into their complex governing configurations. The study encompasses empirical, theoretical, and methodological aspects, shedding light on the governing power of platforms. My PhD research was conducted under the joint supervision of Prof. José van Dijck at Utrecht University and Prof. Carolin Gerlitz at the University of Siegen, Germany.

📧I’m available to (co-)supervise PhD candidates whose research aligns with one or more of my areas of interest.

This focus area promotes research on the social processes of ‘datafication’, ‘platformisation’, and ‘algorithmisation’ to design and apply principles of (good) governance in digital societies. I’m part of a Special Interest Group on ‘Governing the social media and data economy’, which promotes a multidisciplinary approach on social media and app store economics, monetisation models, and data infrastructures.
  • From 2016–2023, I’ve been a researcher in the Collaborative Research Centre ‘Media of Cooperation’ [German: DFG-Sonderforschungsbereich 1187 „Medien der Kooperation”] at the University of Siegen, German, where I’ve been working with Prof. Dr. Carolin Gerlitz (Professor of Digital Media and Methods). This research centre is externally funded by the German Research Foundation (DFG-SFB-1187) for the funding period 2016–2023 and focuses on the study of digitally networked, data-intensive media as conditions for cooperation from multidisciplinary (social, technological, and practice-based) perspectives. As part of a multidisciplinary research team led by Prof. Dr. Carolin Gerlitz (Digital Media and Methods), I help design digital methods and tools to study the material conditions and politics of digital platforms, (mobile) apps, and software and data infrastructures. Additionally, we explore these topics in the study of digital sensory media, especially smartphones and autonomous vehicles. In 2019–2020, I contributed to the succesful application for extending the funding of this research centre for the funding period 2020–2023 (four years). Some of our key research outcomes are published open access in Social Media + Society, again in Social Media + Society, and in Computational Culture (including a special issue on Apps and Infrastructures), in addition to several published working papers and research data sets.
  • In September 2022, I completed and defended ‘The Platform as Ecosystem’, a doctoral (PhD) thesis on the features of platforms’ governance and power. It contributes cutting-edge empirical and historical perspectives on digital ‘platforms’ and the configurations and dynamics of their governance and power. It also explains the need to ‘map’ and study platforms’ larger ‘ecosystems’, especially their considerable business and app ecosystems. The research was jointly supervised by Prof. Dr. José van Dijck (Distinguished University Professor of Media and Digital Society, Utrecht University) and Prof. Dr. Carolin Gerlitz (Professor of Digital Media and Methods, University of Siegen). All of the chapters have been published open access in scholarly (peer-reviewed) journals, including Big Data & Society, Social Media + Society, Internet Policy Review, Internet Histories, TMG – Journal for Media History, and Computational Culture, in addition to several published working papers.
  • From 2020–2021, I conducted a Economic and Social Research Council (ESRC/UKRI)-funded ‘rapid response’ research project on the (role of) mobile apps and app stores in the fight against the coronavirus (COVID-19) pandemic with colleagues from the App Studies Initiative. Some of our key research outcomes are published open access in Internet Policy Review.
  • From 2019–2020, I conducted a Center for Advanced Internet Studies (CAIS)-funded research project on the history and evolution of Facebook’ development platform (including the Facebook Graph and Marketing APIs) with colleagues from the Collaborative Research Centre ‘Media of Cooperation’ [German: DFG-Sonderforschungsbereich 1187 „Medien der Kooperation”] at the University of Siegen. Some of our key research outcomes are published open access in Social Media + Society and the CRC ‘Media of Cooperation’ Working Paper Series.
  • I guest-(co-)edited Apps and Infrastructures, a special issue of Computational Culture from 2019 that has advanced the empirical study of apps and (and as part of) infrastructures.
  • In 2016, I consulted for the European Research Council (ERC)-funded research project ‘Digital Crossings in Europe: Gender, Diaspora and Belonging’ (CONNECTINGEUROPE) (led by Prof. Dr. Sandra Ponzanesi (Professor of Media, Gender and Postcolonial Studies, Utrecht University)) on the use of digital methods and ‘issue mapping’ approaches for studying digital migration and trace transnational digital connections online, across digital platforms and apps. Some of our research outcomes are published open access in Global Networks.
  • From 2014–2015, during my Research Master’s studies at the University of Amsterdam, I assisted Dr. Bernhard Rieder (Associate Professor of New Media and Digital Culture) in his ongoing research on Facebook, YouTube, and the history and applications of ‘algorithmic techniques’. In June 2015, I also completed The Making of Predictions, a Research Master’s (MA) thesis on the use of online search and social media data as grounds (or as a source) for predictive algorithmic techniques. Some of my research from this period is published open access in Big Data & Society and in Surveillance & Society.
  • In June 2013, I completed A Technicity of Collaboration, a Bachelor’s (BA) thesis on the ‘technicity’ of distributed (open-source) software development on GitHub. I graduated with honour (Cum Laude) from the University of Amsterdam in July 2013, partially on the basis of this thesis.
  • In June 2012, I completed Distributed Narratives, a Bachelor’s (BDes) thesis on designing for new (online, digital) narrative forms that were emerging on the Internet. It draws from several ‘visual journalism’ and ‘design research’ projects, including Distributed Images (exhibited at the 2012 Dutch Design Week and in trade journals) and Distributed Identities. The manuscript was edited and planned to be published in paperback.
